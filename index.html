Designer statement:

This website tells the story of my work as a journalist. I wanted the homepage to serve as an introduction, which is why I include a photo of myself, a brief descritption of my specialization and a list of my bylines.

My website then links to my LinkedIn, which the user can use to supplement the material on the site.

My first list is an example of how I write my entertainment-style articles. I organized the page by placing the image and blurb on the same line, as well as adding a holder around text. This makes the individual sections well-defined and easy to read against the background color.

My second list is a re-creation of an article I have written as a food-beat writer. Once again, each blurb is a contained in a holder so that each section is easy to find and read.

My final page is a gallery of personal photos I have taken while on a reporting shift. The photos are organized in the order that someone at the festival would have attended each of the events.

For my entire website, I wanted the text to be casual, yet professional. I avoided unnecessary text styles or decorative fonts.

I wanted the colors on my page to be bright and fun, but not distracting. I used a color picker to complementary colors for my pink background so that there are no issues with visibility. I also only use black for my font colors. All images also include alt-text for users with visual impairment.
